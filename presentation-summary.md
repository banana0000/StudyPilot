# StudyPilot Presentation Summary

Good morning/afternoon everyone.

Today I would like to present StudyPilot, a learning dashboard designed to make studying more organized, focused, and effective.

Many students use several different tools for notes, timers, revision, and task management. StudyPilot brings these activities together in one simple workspace. The application is built with Python, Dash, and Dash Mantine Components, and it works locally without requiring an API key or internet connection.

First, users can upload study materials in PDF, DOCX, or TXT format. The app extracts the text and provides a short summary, helping students understand the main ideas quickly. They can also ask questions about the uploaded material and receive answers based on the relevant content.

StudyPilot also supports active learning. It can generate flashcard prompts and short multiple-choice quizzes from the uploaded document. These features encourage students not only to read their notes, but also to test their understanding and identify topics that need more revision.

Another important feature is the 25-minute focus timer, based on the Pomodoro technique. It helps users work in a focused session, avoid distractions, and take regular breaks. In addition, students can create personal learning goals and track their progress through a checklist.

The dashboard includes visual charts for study rhythm and learning balance, as well as a light and dark mode for a more comfortable experience.

In summary, StudyPilot is a practical and privacy-friendly tool that supports the complete learning process: planning, focusing, reviewing, and checking knowledge. Its main goal is to make learning more visible, structured, and less overwhelming.
